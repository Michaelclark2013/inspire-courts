# Cycle 2 · Phase 6 (Security & Privacy) · 2026-04-25 23:25

## Discovery
1. `/r/[slug]` short-link redirect had no rate limit. The redirect
   itself is harmless, but the click counter feeds attribution
   analytics — without a limit a single bot can inflate counts and
   ruin campaign performance reporting.
2. `/api/admin/sms` POST had no rate limit. Each Twilio outbound
   costs real money. A compromised admin session or runaway client
   loop could rack up a bill before anyone notices.

## Shipped
1. **Per-IP rate limit on `/r/[slug]`** — 60 req/min/IP. Plenty for
   legit users (a person clicking through SMS or scanning a QR), well
   below scraper rates. Returns 429 + `Retry-After: 60` on breach.
2. **Per-admin rate limit on `/api/admin/sms` POST** — 30 sends/min/
   admin. Generous for ad-hoc reply work but bounds the worst case
   at roughly $0.30/min in Twilio fees. Returns 429 + `Retry-After`.
